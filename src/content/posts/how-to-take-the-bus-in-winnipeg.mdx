**Riding the bus for the first time is daunting.**

Going into it without much prior experience is terrifying. I struggled to find resources on how to use this, so I hope this guide serves as a quick run-down on understanding this system!

## Routing a Trip

Use <Link href="https://www.google.com/maps/">Google Maps</Link> for the best experience of bus routing. I was not able to find a better app for bus routing. Sorry, privacy advocates.

Simply put your destination, press "Directions", enter your starting point, then select the transit icon on top.

The Winnipeg Transit app is not great. It may be worth downloading it anyway to refer to schedule times for bus stops. You can also use the <Link href="https://app.winnipegtransit.com/">web app</Link>.

### The bus isn't here, what happened?

Sometimes, the bus was early and passed by you. There are also times where buses may get cancelled. This can be incredibly annoying.

Winnipeg Transit and Google Maps can tell you when the next bus will come. You can also call the <Link href="tel:2042877433">TeleBUS number</Link> that's put on every bus stop sign, to figure out when your bus will arrive too.

### What happens if I get lost?

If you have mobile data, simply reroute from your location to where you want to go. If you don't, see if there's any public Wi-Fi nearby.

You may want to come up with an excuse to parents of how you might've gotten lost, if you're transporting yourself to a place that you'd rather keep private.

You may also wish to ask strangers for help. Admittedly, it's a bit scary, but it's better than being stranded. If you just realized you made a wrong turn, you can also talk to the bus driver.

A strategy for if you get lost is to stay on said bus, until you wrap around to your previous location. Otherwise, getting off may be an option if you know you can walk to the next stop or your destination.

## Fares

Taking the bus (unfortunately) costs money.

Winnipeg Transit takes either of the following methods of payment:

- Cash (no change is given!)
- Bus tickets
- Peggo card

To get bus tickets or a Peggo card, check out <Link href="https://info.winnipegtransit.com/en/fares/where-to-buy/">Where to Buy</Link>, and find the closest place near you.

To pay, walk to the front door of your bus, and then you'll see a fare box.

- **Cash:** There will be a little slot on the right to insert coins. Pay in exact change, as the bus driver does not carry change. Otherwise, you'll overpay (more than you already are by paying with cash; it's more expensive than tickets!).
- **Bus tickets:** The same little slot on the right is used to accept bus tickets.
- **Peggo card:** The farebox will have a rectangular red scanner. Simply tap the red scanner to pay, and you'll hear a beep confirming your payment.

Once paid either by bus ticket or cash, you should receive a **paper transfer**. This is important, but we'll get to that soon though!

If you're strapped on money, there's programs for reduced fares. If you're a student, you can take advantage of the Youth Fare pricing. If you're eligible and paying with cash, you should carry your photo ID. You may also want to check out <Link href="https://info.winnipegtransit.com/en/fares/winnpass">WINNpass</Link>, especially if you're on <dfn id="eia">Employment and Income Assistance</dfn> (<abbr>EIA</abbr>).

For more info and current pricing, check out <Link href="https://info.winnipegtransit.com/en/fares/transit-fares/">Transit Fares</Link>.

## Transfers

### Paper Transfers

If you're using bus tickets, once you insert your ticket in the slot, the bus driver should give you a paper transfer. Same goes with cash.

**Make sure to take your paper transfer!**

The reason you want to have this transfer, is then you'll be able to take another bus without having to spend another ticket for a period of time.

To reach your destination, you may need to take multiple buses to get there. You can avoid spending another ticket, by putting the transfer's QR code face-first on the red scanner.

You'll notice that your transfer has a date and time issued, and how much time until it expires. Once it expires, you'll need to use another ticket, as the machine won't accept it any longer.

If it expired recently though (say, 15 minutes), and you have one more bus, you can ask the bus driver to let you on anyway.

If for some reason the driver hasn't given you a transfer, simply ask.

### Peggo Cards

If you're using a Peggo card, you won't receive a paper transfer. Instead, your card will now act as a transfer.

It won't charge you again when changing buses, for a period of time. You can verify this by changing buses and scanning your Peggo card as normal. The blue display will tell you when your transfer will expire.

## What are BLUE buses?

BLUE buses are part of a rapid transit line, which come by fairly frequently.

These buses will have names in amber text, such as "St. Norbert" and "University of Manitoba".
