**Want to know how to get good at any skill easily?**

You know how some of you just dump all your time in video games? Just do that, but for the thing you want to get good at.

Except, there's a problem. It doesn't work like that, right? That's because video games are fun. And it's hard to put all your time into something that isn't fun.

And there's the secret. **Play, have fun!**

## Challenges Towards Fun

This requires you to have a change of mindset to certain things that you may struggle with. Let's say for example, is that you want to make really good art.

If you're primarily focused on results, you'll probably have a bad time. When starting out with anything new, more likely than not, you _will_ suck. Especially if you have rejection sensitivity, this can be rough. ~~Hence why my art skills are lacking.~~

Alternatively, there's being focused on if you're able to do a very specific thing. Something like "make good art" is way too broad, and needs to be broken down.

Rather, a more small, focused goal could be practicing how to use a drawing tablet, and make straight lines. You could make that more specific, such as defining an amount of time you want to do it.

When you're able to do small goals, you'll get a dopamine hit! This helps you train your brain to keep doing things that give you dopamine.

## The Positive Cycle

Once you find the fun, it's easy to perpetuate.

You probably know the traditional advice of practicing regularly and staying persistant. While you're having fun, all this will be natural. Need a schedule to force you to do the thing? Not anymore. You'll _want_ to do it.

Over time, you're going to put more and more hours, and you'll become better and better.

## Conclusion

**Make learning fun for yourself!** Not only will you get better at the skill you want, but you'll also be able to enjoy doing it in the process.
